Hurricane Defense is a division in Restorever to combat hurricanes by disrupting hurricane formation. Hurricanes are formed over seawater from rising moist air from hot sea surfaces, generating thunderstorms with a low pressure center and low vertical wind shear. Hurricanes require the Coriolis force for formation. Hurricanes are exacerbated by rising sea surface temperatures caused by climate change.

Hurricane Defense is conducting a feasibility study on hurricane disruption using computational fluid dynamics (1). This report will inform further experimental tests with the permission of relevant government agencies. Each hurricane causes $100-120 billion in damages, evident in hurricanes Milton (2024) and Helene (2024) in USA. Restorever aims to charge $1 billion per hurricane to defend nations against them.
